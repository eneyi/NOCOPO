About the initiative:
Over the past decade, there has been a global movement with different initiatives (public, private and donor-led) focusing on opening up contracting with the main aim of fighting corruption, fostering transparency and encouraging participatory governance.
Public projects such as health, education, infrastructure and agriculture, often drive the development agenda of most governments, however given the amounts involved during government contracting, corruption becomes the government’s biggest risk. 15% of global GDP is spent annually on government contracts amounting to $9.5 trillion, while in Nigeria public procurement in 2015 totalled x amount. Their successful implementation and achievement of project objectives is predicted by, among other things, on the public procurement process: planning, supplier selection and contract management, that is transparent and open to ensure funds are absorbed into specified projects and the government is accountable to its citizens.

Objectives of the initiative

  • One stop-shop for public contract information
  • Prevent fraud and corruption & enhance transparency in public procurement
  • Monitor contract implementation to ensure accountability
  • Better value for money for the governmen
  • Inclusivity for local firms (including SMEs) in government contracting
